# Community Etiquette

IRC is asynchronous. People aren't always at their keyboard. These norms keep the place liveable.

---

## The basics

**Don't ask to ask.** Don't open with "can anyone help me?" — just ask the question. Someone who knows will respond when they see it.

**Idle is fine.** Leaving your client connected 24/7 is normal on IRC. You don't need to announce when you're leaving or arriving.

**No flooding.** Don't paste large blocks of text directly into a channel. Use a pastebin (paste.rs, 0x0.st, etc.) and share the link.

**Stay on topic (loosely).** `#stellar` is general, `#releases` is releases, `#audiophile` is gear. Wander within reason.

**No spam, no bots** (without staff approval). Automated messages, advertising, or unsolicited DMs will get you banned.

---

## Tone

This is a private community. Being here is a privilege of membership, not a right. Treat people as you'd want to be treated in a small room with a closed door.

- No harassment, targeted mockery, or pile-ons.
- Disagreements about music, gear, or code are fine. Disagreements about people are not.
- Staff have final say. If you think a decision was wrong, DM a staff member rather than arguing in channel.

---

## Staff contacts

Reach staff via `#staff` (post a message, they'll see it) or by opening a support ticket through Stellar's private messaging system.

---

## Kicks and bans

| Action              | Consequence                              |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Minor disruption    | Verbal warning in channel                |
| Repeated disruption | Kick, then temporary ban (hours to days) |
| Serious violation   | Permanent ban + Stellar account review   |

Bans on IRC may trigger account review on Stellar at staff discretion.
